Here are the Pacific island nations that still back Taiwan. Each line is based on grants disclosed by each nation in their annual budgets.

Palau should be on here too (in green), but their records are complicated, and will take more time to sort through. The Guardian reported in 2018 (with no attribution) that they're sent $10 million a year, but let's take that with a big pinch of salt.

The money they get is similar to the grants the PRC gives to neighboring Kiribati and the Solomon Islands, who both switched sides in 2019.

The only numbers published in their financial records are an $11.7 million grant to the Solomon Islands in 2021...

...and a $10.2 million one to Kiribati in 2022.

The PRC rarely offers grants, preferring loans that can be paid back. Despite being the world's second-largest economy, the grants it is willing to give are no bigger than tiny Taiwan's.
















Methodology:

Grants: Grants have been defined in this investigation as any money either donated by Taiwan to a diplomatic ally, or else used to fund a local development project free of charge.

Exchange Rate: All figures from Tuvalu and Kiribati were converted from AUD to USD using x-rates.com, on January 1st of each year. Reports from Nauru provided their own exchange rates which were followed accordingly.

Forecasts: The only figures available for Tuvalu for 2017-2018, and 2021-2022, were forecasts from 2016 and 2020 respectively, and may not represent the actual sum given that year.
